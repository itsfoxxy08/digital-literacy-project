# 💳 UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information or authorizing fraudulent transactions. This type of fraud usually targets individuals who actively use digital payment apps.

A typical scenario involves a fraudster calling or messaging the victim, pretending to be a bank official or a customer support executive. They may ask the user to click on a malicious link or request them to approve a payment request instead of receiving money. In some cases, attackers send fake QR codes or links that redirect users to phishing websites.

Once the victim unknowingly shares OTPs, UPI PINs, or approves a payment request, money is instantly transferred from their account to the attacker. These transactions are often difficult to reverse.

Students and new users of digital payment systems are particularly vulnerable due to lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and compromised personal data.

This case study highlights the importance of awareness and careful usage of digital payment systems to avoid falling victim to such scams.
